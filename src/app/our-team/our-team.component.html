Leo Roca is a born-and-raised Washingtonian, having lived the past 30-years in the D.C. metropolitan area. Leo obtained a Bachelor of Arts in Political Science from George Mason University and his law degree from American University’s Washington College of Law. During college and law school, Leo worked in numerous law firms in the DMV area and comes to our firm from a local firm in Rockville, Maryland where he worked on civil litigation matters. Leo takes great pride in his interaction with clients and his managerial role within Metropolitan Legal Solutions. Getting to know each client as an individual with distinct needs and concerns helps him craft personalized strategies to effectively litigate their personal injury cases. “In my experience, a one-size-fits-all approach to problem solving never helps anyone.” An active listener who prides himself on his communication skills, Leo is adept at familiarizing his clients with the process of litigation and guiding them confidently through it. Recognizing that injured clients are in distress and that time often is of the essence, Leo commits himself to establishing a trusting relationship with each of his clients, anticipating their needs, and then promptly addressing every concern they may have. Leo's experience and his disarming demeanor brings a sense of calm to his clients, their cases, and even to the courtroom. In his free time, Leo enjoys trying new restaurants around the D.C. area.

Raymond was born and raised in San Juan, Porto Rico. Before joining Metropolitan Legal Services, Raymond graduated from Loyola University with a Bachelor’s degree in Accounting and Information Systems. Prior to attending law school, Raymond worked as a Corporate Paralegal at Cleary Gottlieb Steen & Hamilton in New York City, where he mostly assisted the Capital Markets and Corporate Practice Group. During his time at the American University Washington College of Law, Raymond worked for Pietrantoni Mendez & Alvarez, an international law firm in San Juan, Porto Rico, assisting the Litigation and Corporate Practice Group. Raymond’s other law school activities included being a member of the International Law Review, an Editor of the Academy on Human Rights and Humanitarian Law Book, and a Student Clinician in the Human Rights Clinic. Through his Law Review comment, Raymond acquired an interest in the Space, Satellite, and Aviation law fields. Before joining our firm, Raymond worked for SES World Skies, a company that provides satellite communications to the aviation and maritime industry, and for the Federal Communications Commission’s Satellite Division. Raymond’s extensive background and experience in aviation law makes him an asset to the aviation practice at Metropolitan Legal Solutions. When he is not working, Raymond enjoys following the New York Yankees and spending time with his family.
